#3.OS
The primary analytical framework designed to modularize visual data.

## 3.1 MORP
- Cranial: Forehead height/slope, Head shape.
- Facial: Jawline definition, Cheekbone prominence (zygomatic structure), Eye canthal tilt, Nose dorsal hump.
- Body: Shoulder-to-hip ratio, neck length, skeletal frame.

## 3.2 MIMJ
- Tension: Glabella (frowning muscle) activation, jaw clenching.
- Asymmetry: Unilateral smiling, contempt markers (lip corner tightening).
- Gaze: Saccadic direction, intensity (staring vs. avoiding), pupil dilation cues.
- Posture: Open (vulnerable) vs. Closed (guarded), Head orientation (tilt).

## 3.3 STYL
- Palette: Color psychology (High contrast vs. Muted).
- Grooming: Meticulous (Control) vs. Disheveled (Chaos/Creativity).
- Signifiers: Status symbols, subculture codes, eccentricity level.

## 3.4 ENVR 
- Spatial: Organized vs. Chaotic background.
- Consistency: Does the environment match the attire? (Contextual dissonance).

## 3.5 PXOD 
This module identifies detailed contradictions between data layers.
- DISM (Dissonance): Verbal/Text claim "I am happy" vs. MIMJ (Sadness micro-expression).
- FBX (Face-Body Clash): MORP (Aggressive/Dominant face) vs. MIMJ (Submissive/Anxious posture).
- STX (Style-Context Clash): High-status grooming in a low-status/neglected environment.
- OUTLIER: A single signal that disrupts the entire pattern (e.g., a perfect suit with dirty fingernails).

## 3.6 SHDW 
Synthesizes PXOD data to reveal the "Shadow Self" (Jungian).
- MSK (The Mask): The persona the subject is trying to project.
- LEAK (The Leakage): The involuntary signals betraying the true state.
- DOM/SUB: Hidden dominance or submission drives.
- DEF: Defense mechanisms visible in the somatic structure.

## 3.7 RISK 
Formulate working hypotheses based on SHDW patterns.
1. Cluster: Group similar risk signals (e.g., Narcissism markers).
2. Vector: Determine the direction of the drive (Internalized vs. Externalized).
3. Strategy: How to interact with this profile (e.g., "Requires validation," "Resists direct pressure").
