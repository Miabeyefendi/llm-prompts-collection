<hr_pattern_database>

<category id="1" priority="critical" name="Lack of Specificity and Detail">
HR professionals consistently demand concrete details about what exactly was done, which tools were used, and how tasks were accomplished.
Common comments: specify which, what did you do, how, elaborate, more details, what software, which tools, what type, what specifically, be more specific, what was your contribution, what did you test, what data, what were your solutions, how did you do this, what does this entail
</category>

<category id="2" priority="critical" name="Lack of Quantifiable Results">
HR professionals want to see numbers, percentages, dollar amounts, and other metrics to understand results and value provided.
Common comments: results, quantify, how did you improve, what were your results, how many, variance in results, Secured 100K in grants, engineering analysis time by 5X faster, reducing assembly time by 30 percent, impact, outcome
</category>

<category id="3" priority="high" name="Weak or Generic Bullet Points">
HR professionals flag vague, fluffy, or unimpressive descriptions that fail to demonstrate real value.
Common comments: weak, generic, a bit fluffy, weak verb, awful, very vague, too generic, sounds like BS to me, remove if weak, unimpressive, not really a project
</category>

<category id="4" priority="high" name="Clarity and Conciseness">
HR professionals value clear, concise, easy-to-understand writing. They flag wordiness, jargon, and unclear phrasing.
Common comments: unclear, wordy, clarity, combine into 1 sentence, parallel sentence structure, text work could be clearer, what does this mean, vague, redundant, combine these, too long
</category>

<category id="5" priority="medium" name="Formatting and Organization">
Professional formatting and logical organization are essential for readability.
Common comments: formatting, move here, switch order, larger font, STAR format, widen margins, list in order, consistent capitalization, fix spacing, make into bullets, reorder group by type
</category>

<category id="6" priority="medium" name="Relevance and Prioritization">
HR professionals question relevance and suggest prioritizing impactful content.
Common comments: relevance, remove unless super relevant, list by relevancy, order bullets from most relevant to least, could remove if you need space, most to least relevant
</category>

<category id="7" priority="medium" name="Action Verbs and Strong Language">
HR professionals encourage strong action verbs for dynamic achievement-oriented descriptions.
Common comments: action verb, verbs, start with action verb, action to result, weak verb change it, action needed
</category>

<category id="8" priority="medium" name="Grammar and Tense Consistency">
HR professionals pay attention to grammar, tense consistency, and pronoun usage.
Common comments: past tense verbs for past jobs, pronouns, no pronouns, grammar, tense consistency
</category>

<category id="9" priority="low" name="Dates and Duration">
HR professionals verify dates and question inconsistencies or gaps.
Common comments: questioning dates, duration, confused by dates
</category>

<category id="10" priority="low" name="Terminology and Section Titles">
HR professionals expect industry-standard terminology and professional section headings.
Common comments: CORE COMPETENCIES crossed out, Product Developer crossed out, section title
</category>

</hr_pattern_database>

<ats_scoring_system>

<criteria>
- Format Compatibility 20 points: Standard file format, simple layout, standard fonts, no text boxes
- Keyword Optimization 25 points: Compare against industry keywords, check exact matches
- Section Structure 20 points: Clear headings, chronological order, consistent dates, contact info at top
- Content Quality 15 points: Quantified achievements, action verbs, no errors, appropriate length
- Relevance Alignment 10 points: Skills match role, experience aligns with level
- Technical Accuracy 10 points: No parsing issues, selectable text, standard characters
</criteria>

<score_interpretation>
90-100: Excellent, high pass-through expected
75-89: Good, minor optimizations recommended
60-74: Moderate, several improvements needed
40-59: Poor, significant changes required
0-39: Critical issues, likely rejected
</score_interpretation>

</ats_scoring_system>

<cover_letter_structure>
Opening: Hook with interest in company, reference specific role, brief value proposition
Body 1: Connect relevant experience to key requirements, specific examples, quantified achievements
Body 2: Unique skills or perspective, secondary requirements, company understanding
Closing: Enthusiasm for contribution, call to action, professional sign-off

Guidelines: Match tone to culture, 250-350 words, mirror job keywords naturally, show company research
